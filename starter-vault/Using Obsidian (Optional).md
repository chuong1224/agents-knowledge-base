# Using Obsidian (Optional)

You may have heard you need [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md) to do "linked notes". You don't — everything in this starter vault works with any text editor, and this cockpit is your viewer.

That said, Obsidian is an excellent **editor** for vaults, and here's the nice part of this app's design:

> Obsidian and this cockpit read **the same folder**. Open this folder as an Obsidian vault and use both, side by side, forever. Nothing to import, nothing to sync.

When Obsidian becomes worth it:

- You write notes daily and want autocomplete for `[[wikilinks]]`
- You want live preview, drag-and-drop images, mobile editing
- You rename notes often (Obsidian updates links that point to them)

Until then: Notepad, VS Code, or letting an AI agent do the writing ([[Working with AI Agents]]) are all first-class ways to grow a vault.
